# Hades Watch Text MMO: Expanded Play Functions

## Purpose

This document defines the next wave of playable functions for the Hades Watch text MMO. It expands the first playable loop foundation into daily, weekly, cooperative, creative, archival, profile-centered, and community stewardship play.

The goal is to make Hades Watch feel alive day-to-day without making combat the center of progression. Players enter the Underwatch, choose a small safe action, leave a Field Log, earn identity-forward rewards, and slowly change the rooms and communities around them.

This reference is canonical gameplay and content source material for future seeding, reward mapping, admin review flows, faction stewardship, room-state design, avatar unlocks, badges, titles, and reputation events. It contains no implementation code.

## Safety and Nonviolence Policy

All expanded gameplay must be fictional, legal, nonviolent, privacy-aware, inclusive, moderated, safe for community play, accessibility-aware, old-web creative, and community-centered.

Do not reward violence, weapons, combat, harassment, doxxing, stalking, intimidation, sabotage, illegal hacking, malware, exploit instructions, credential theft, evasion, real-world targeting, unsafe protest tactics, unsafe medical/legal advice, private data exposure, extremist content, or harmful real-world coordination.

Do reward safe lore, puzzle solving, community support, accessibility notes, public-interest resources, archive reading, forum participation, documentation, repair culture, self-hosting education, profile creativity, avatar customization, guild stewardship, player recognition, faction identity, old-web creativity, nonviolent civic imagination, privacy literacy, and mutual aid imagination without operational risk.

Any tech, signal, cipher, or Oracular Circuit gameplay must remain fictional, puzzle-based, educational, defensive, or privacy-literacy oriented. No real-world hacking objectives. Use the label **Fictional Props & Tech Gear** and do not use “weapons” as an avatar or loot category.

## Existing Gameplay Foundation

Expanded functions connect to the existing Field Ops foundation: MMO Hub / Field Ops, Rooms, Underwatch Town Square, Dead Drop Terminal, Oracular Relay, Faction Floors, Archive Terminal, Dead Drops, Field Logs, Badge Case, Relic Zone, Profile World, Character Dossier, Avatar Builder, Community Builder, Forums, Guilds, Volunteer Board, Reputation, Canon Tiers of Lore, Player Recognition, Archive, Ghost in Tech, State of Affairs, Signal Player, and Net Neighbors.

These functions should not create isolated gameplay. A Daily Signal can point to a room. A Forum Quest can unlock a Profile World frame. A Guild Project can stabilize a room state. An Archive Hunt can reveal a lore trace. A Mentor Guide can unlock public recognition. The Underwatch grows sideways through rooms, threads, guild cells, relic shelves, and opt-in creative traces.

## Expanded Gameplay Design Pillars

### 1. Small Actions, Persistent Meaning

Players should be able to complete small actions that leave lasting traces: reputation, badges, Field Logs, profile items, room state changes, lore unlocks, and community recognition.

### 2. Repeatable Without Becoming Grind

Daily and weekly loops should be lightweight and meaningful, not spammy. Use cooldowns, caps, review gates, and no-guilt rest signals.

### 3. Creativity Is Progression

Forum posts, lore fragments, profile themes, guild rituals, archive summaries, banner designs, and avatar concepts count as gameplay when safe and reviewed.

### 4. Community Is the Map

The world expands through rooms, threads, guilds, Net Neighbors, archive entries, and player-created lore.

### 5. Loot Is Identity

Rewards unlock titles, avatar accessories, relics, badges, profile cosmetics, banner parts, Signal Player skins, and faction flair. Loot does not grant combat power.

### 6. Safety Is a Game Mechanic

Safe play, privacy awareness, accessibility, and harm reduction are Underwatch doctrine and progression paths.

### 7. Admin / Steward Review Protects the World

Higher-impact submissions require review before public display or reward grants.

## Function 1: Daily Signals

**Slug:** daily-signals  
**Primary Route Suggestion:** /mmo/daily-signals  
**Connected Systems:** Rooms, Field Logs, Reputation, Profile World, Archive, Ciphers  
**Primary Play Type:** tiny daily prompt  
**Access Level:** approved-user  
**Repeatability:** daily  
**Review Requirement:** mostly automatic; review for submitted notes  
**MVP Priority:** phase-1  

### Gameplay Purpose

This function gives operatives a safe, repeatable way to participate in the Underwatch without combat or pressure-grind design.

### Lore Framing

The Underwatch routes this function through terminal rooms, guild cells, faction floors, and the Dead Index as a controlled signal rather than a command. Participation is a lantern, not a leash.

### Player Fantasy

The player feels like an operative whose small actions matter: reading, writing, reflecting, helping, archiving, building, tuning, guiding, or preserving memory.

### Core Player Actions

- Read the active prompt or room state.
- Choose a safe action.
- Submit a small response, completion marker, or reviewed contribution.
- Receive a Field Log, reward hook, or room-state contribution.
- Move toward badges, titles, avatar unlocks, lore, or public recognition.

### Example Activities

- **Static Check-In:** Name one safe thing your operative notices in the static today.
- **Read the Signal Board:** Read one active Signal Board item and mark it remembered.
- **One-Sentence Field Note:** Leave one sentence from your room, guild cell, or profile world.
- **Archive Candle:** Read one Archive item or lore fragment.
- **Cipher Tap:** Attempt one fictional cipher or request a hint.

### Reward Types

Reputation, badges, player titles, avatar unlocks, relic fragments, profile cosmetics, forum flair, guild banners, Signal Player skins, room access marks, lore unlocks, and recognition templates.

### Badge Hooks

Badge hooks should be milestone-based, capped where repeatable, and revoked if the underlying public contribution is removed for safety reasons.

### Avatar Unlock Hooks

Avatar rewards use accessories, faction flair, back items, effects, backgrounds, and **Fictional Props & Tech Gear**. They are identity rewards, not combat tools.

### Reputation Events

Reputation should be small for automatic actions and larger for reviewed contributions. Daily and weekly caps prevent spam farming.

### Field Log Examples

- `[callsign] completed a Daily Signals signal.`
- `[callsign] left a safe trace in the Underwatch.`
- `[callsign] earned progress toward a recognition mark.`

### Admin / Moderator Needs

Review public or high-impact submissions; approve, reject, request revision, hide unsafe content, grant or revoke rewards, and record review notes.

### Faction Leader Needs

Faction leaders may draft or steward low-risk faction-specific prompts and recommend rewards from approved reward pools. They cannot bypass admin safety review or grant Owner-only marks.

### Safety Notes

Do not log sensitive content. Do not create real-world targeting objectives. Do not reward spam. Preserve opt-in privacy and accessibility defaults.

### Implementation Metadata

- stable_slug: `daily-signals`
- field_log_required: true
- reward_pool_required: true
- review_queue_supported: `mostly automatic; review for submitted notes`
- privacy_default: approved-users or private where appropriate

## Admin and Moderator Review Needs

Review is required for weekly faction call submissions, public works task completions, player-created micro-missions, guild projects, profile world challenge showcases, archive hunts that include submitted resources, forum quests with public recognition, mentor/guide status, public recognition, and seasonal event submissions.

Admin and moderator tools should support approve, reject, request revision, grant rewards, revoke rewards, mark content hidden, mark content featured, add review notes, and audit reward grants. Public display and reward grants should be separable: a submission may be safe enough for private completion but not appropriate for public featuring.

## Faction Leader Review Needs

Faction leaders may draft faction calls, review faction-specific low-risk submissions when assigned, recommend rewards from approved pools, propose faction event prompts, propose faction reward pool additions, and steward faction guide threads.

Faction leaders cannot grant Owner-only marks, assign Chthonic Uprising recognition, approve World Canon, create arbitrary rewards, bypass admin safety review, approve their own rewards, or publish unsafe missions.

# 1. Heathen

## Dead Index ID

`DI-0000-HEATHEN`

## Public Handle

**Heathen**

## Known Aliases

- The Archivist
- Slewfoot
- Keeper of the Dead Index
- Warden of the Five Cells
- Sovereign of the Underwatch, used by others more than by Heathen
- Chthonic Overlord, disputed title, mostly admin flavor and surface fear-myth

## Faction Alignment

**The Chthonic Uprising**

## Rank or Function

Founder, coordinator, memory-keeper, archive-builder, signal witch, technopunk technomancer, reluctant leader.

Heathen maps mythically to the site Owner role, but in lore this authority is framed as custodianship, not kingship.

## Profile Summary

Heathen is the underworld’s living index of erased people, broken systems, buried evidence, and unfinished promises. They do not command like a king. They route, verify, preserve, refuse, retaliate, and remember.

They became a symbol because too many people needed one place to send the impossible. Heathen would rather be a maintenance tunnel than a throne.

Core line:

> I am a child of the underworld who dreams of sunlight.

## Appearance and Presence

Heathen looks like someone who has slept beside machines for too long and still listens to ghosts in the fan noise. Their coat is patched with black pomegranate thread, signal tags, broken laurel pins, and warding glyphs printed in conductive ink. Their cyberdeck is old, ugly, sacred, and repaired beyond manufacturer shame. One boot drags slightly, giving rise to the name Slewfoot.

They do not glow like a chosen one. They flicker like bad power in a locked clinic.

## Backstory

Before the Uprising had a name, Heathen was collecting fragments.

Denied care logs. Lost court records. Eviction footage. Police heat maps. worker injury denials. Scrubbed articles. Last messages. Vanished case files. Memorial lists mislabeled as fraud clusters. Heathen built the first Dead Index out of terror, guilt, spite, and a need to prove the disappeared had existed.

The first mistake was trying to save everything.

The first lesson was consent.

Dr. Ione Vey taught Heathen that privacy is medicine. Cassian Nyx taught them that records without threat models become traps. Brontes Vale taught them that truth needs logistics. Mara Kallix taught them that every archive needs exits. Rhea Spite taught them that grief needs walls to scream on.

Heathen did not create the Uprising alone. The Uprising grew because nurses, punks, dockworkers, coders, disabled organizers, engineers, signal ghosts, broke students, burned-out parents, and the half-deleted kept showing up with proof and refusing to vanish.

## Escapades

### The Black Pomegranate Copy

Heathen smuggled the first full Dead Index backup through a biometric checkpoint hidden inside a devotional fruit crate. The pomegranates were real. The seeds were not. Each seed held a shard of deleted mortality data, wrapped in devotional metadata so old it confused the Census.

### The Night of 10,000 Names

When the Surface Order erased a public memorial roll as “civic disinfo,” Heathen coordinated a distributed resurrection. Names appeared on bus kiosks, clinic printers, smart fridges, drone ads, closed school tablets, payroll systems, and one luxury tower fountain.

The Surface called it data vandalism.

The underworld called it attendance.

### The Apology Lock

After exposing a witness too early and nearly getting them taken, Heathen built the Apology Lock, a Dead Index protocol that requires consent state, exposure risk, faction review, and harm assessment before sensitive records can be surfaced.

The lock remains one of the few protocols Heathen refuses to override alone.

## Weaknesses and Tensions

- Over-responsibility
- Secrecy as reflex
- Insomnia
- Grief hoarding
- Fear of becoming what they fight
- Difficulty accepting care
- Distrust of titles
- Tendency to disappear into the archive when people need a body in the room

## Faction Relationships

- **Asclepian Veil:** Heathen owes them their life and resents being told to sleep.
- **Oracular Circuit:** Deep trust, frequent arguments over acceptable risk.
- **Myrmidon Grinders:** Protective bond built from evacuation failures and saved routes.
- **Daedalus Foundry:** Shared obsession with impossible infrastructure.
- **Styx Rats:** Rhea calls Heathen “graveyard modem.” Heathen pretends not to like it.

## Dossier Quote

> Do not let them turn your dead into missing data. Do not let them turn your living into risk scores. We remember with teeth.

# 2. Dr. Ione Vey

## Dead Index ID

`DI-0101-IONE-VEY`

## Public Handle

**Dr. Ione Vey**

## Known Aliases

- Veil-Cantor
- Wound Archivist
- Panacea Handler
- The Doctor Under the Floor
- Saint of Bad Paperwork, Styx Rat nickname

## Faction Alignment

**The Asclepian Veil**

## Rank or Function

Founder and leader of the Asclepian Veil. Former biomedical compliance officer turned underground care strategist.

## Profile Summary

Dr. Ione Vey leads the Uprising’s healers, medics, harm-reduction workers, field stabilizers, privacy advocates, and underclinic operators.

She is calm in a way that scares people who confuse panic with seriousness. She believes survival is the first sabotage and privacy is medicine.

## Appearance and Presence

Ione wears a dark underclinic coat cut like a surgeon’s gown and a mourning veil, lined with emergency pomegranate red. Her diagnostic gloves display no corporate logo. A serpent-and-fiberoptic staff sigil is tattooed along her wrist in green surgical light. She keeps her hair pinned with bone-white clips shaped like tiny splints.

Her voice lowers the room’s heart rate.

## Backstory

Ione was trained to optimize care. That was the phrase.

She worked inside a biomedical compliance ministry where care models decided who was “recoverable,” who was “resource-negative,” and who could be left to administrative silence. She audited populations the Surface Order called nonviable.

Then she found the mortality tables.

The system was not failing. It was selecting.

Dissent clusters. Disabled people. addicts. queer patients. elders. unpaid workers. people who missed rent. people whose social graphs touched mutual aid. people who had protested. people who had simply become expensive.

Ione leaked the tables to Heathen, walked out with three med caches, and vanished beneath a laundromat where the dryers masked the sound of the first Veil clinic generator.

## Escapades

### The Laundromat Clinic

The first Asclepian Veil clinic ran behind six broken dryers, two working machines, and a wall of detergent boxes that hid a triage room. Patients entered with laundry bags and left with antibiotics, wound care, hormones, food vouchers, sleep plans, and their metadata scrubbed.

### The Denial Reversal

Ione forged an entire week of algorithmic approvals by exploiting an obsolete hospice billing pathway. The Surface Order later called it “mass resource misdirection.” The Veil called it Tuesday.

### The Soft Quarantine

During a Census sweep, Ione declared a false infectious hazard inside a housing block to keep private security out long enough for the Grinders to evacuate blacklisted families.

No one got sick.

That was the miracle.

## Weaknesses and Tensions

- Carries guilt for her years inside the compliance system
- Can become clinically detached under stress
- Distrusts spectacle
- Overprotects patients and sometimes withholds operational information
- Clashes with Grinders who romanticize injury
- Clashes with Rats when jokes risk vulnerable people

## Faction Relationships

- **Heathen:** Patient, friend, liability. Ione has override authority on Heathen’s rest protocols, whether Heathen admits it or not.
- **Oracular Circuit:** Essential for privacy tools and metadata hygiene.
- **Myrmidon Grinders:** Loved and scolded in equal measure.
- **Daedalus Foundry:** Closest operational partner. Assistive tech, med devices, portable clinics.
- **Styx Rats:** Useful morale vectors, occasional infection-control nightmare.

## Dossier Quote

> A living body is not a rounding error. Patch the wound. Encrypt the chart. Feed the patient. Then we can talk about revolution.

# 3. Cassian Nyx

## Dead Index ID

`DI-0201-CASSIAN-NYX`

## Public Handle

**Cassian Nyx**

## Known Aliases

- Pythian Root
- Oracle-Breaker
- Fate Spoofer
- Static Prophet
- The Kid the Model Missed

## Faction Alignment

**The Oracular Circuit**

## Rank or Function

Founder and leader of the Oracular Circuit. Cryptographer, signal analyst, AI whisperer, hostile prophecy breaker.

## Profile Summary

Cassian Nyx learned the language of predictive systems before they learned mercy. The Surface Order treated algorithms like gods and citizens like weather. Cassian treats every oracle as a machine with a throat.

They do not worship prophecy. They debug it.

## Appearance and Presence

Cassian dresses in layered black glass fabric with violet reflective seams and cyan circuit embroidery. Their eyes are always tracking something slightly off-screen. Their fingers twitch through invisible keyboards. A cracked Delphi tripod symbol hangs from their collar, its legs made of conductive wire.

They speak quietly because the machines are always listening.

## Backstory

Cassian grew up inside a predictive policing lab. Their caregivers were researchers, handlers, mentors, wardens, and priests of the model. They were told the system could see danger before it happened. They were told bias was an obsolete human superstition. They were told prediction was compassion at scale.

Then the model predicted the death of someone Cassian loved.

The prediction summoned the patrol pattern that made the death likely. Cassian watched the oracle create its own proof.

They broke the model. Not with rage. With comprehension.

Cassian stole training data, corrupted validation sets, copied vendor contracts, and followed one malformed output string into the first Dead Index node. Heathen found them half-starved, laughing at a police dashboard that had started routing patrol cars into ornamental ponds.

## Escapades

### The Six-Hour Blindfold

Cassian spoofed a Census district into reporting low-risk devotional shopping behavior while the Veil evacuated blacklisted patients. The model was not destroyed. It was lulled.

Six hours. Twenty-nine lives.

### The Oracle That Lied Back

During an intrusion into a corporate prophecy engine, Cassian received a message that appeared to predict their own betrayal of the Uprising. Cassian published the message internally, then built a training exercise around it called **Never Trust a God That Knows Your Fear.**

### The Saint of Bad Metadata

Cassian once saved an entire Rat graffiti crew by flooding their social graph with AI-generated aunties, fake choir schedules, devotional coupon habits, and one imaginary ferret rescue collective.

## Weaknesses and Tensions

- Hypervigilance
- Difficulty trusting human intuition over signal evidence
- Can overfit emotionally, especially around loss
- Tends to treat uncertainty as a personal failure
- Clashes with Styx Rats over loud action
- Clashes with Foundry over deploying tools before perfect threat modeling

## Faction Relationships

- **Heathen:** Shared insomnia. Shared paranoia. Shared refusal to call machines gods.
- **Asclepian Veil:** Provides privacy tooling, secure charting, route obfuscation.
- **Myrmidon Grinders:** Respects their discipline, fears their predictability.
- **Daedalus Foundry:** Productive friction. Cassian secures what Mara insists must ship.
- **Styx Rats:** Frequent tactical conflict, secret admiration.

## Dossier Quote

> The oracle does not see the future. It prices your options until only one door is left. Break the hinge.

# 4. Brontes Vale

## Dead Index ID

`DI-0301-BRONTES-VALE`

## Public Handle

**Brontes Vale**

## Known Aliases

- Gatebreaker
- Cerberus Marshal
- Line Holder
- The Dock Saint
- Brontes of Dock Nine

## Faction Alignment

**The Myrmidon Grinders**

## Rank or Function

Founder and leader of the Myrmidon Grinders. Defender, logistics commander, protest marshal, evacuation planner.

## Profile Summary

Brontes Vale leads the people who show up when theory gets cornered. The Grinders are not mindless muscle. They are discipline, logistics, defense, supply, evacuation, crowd safety, and strength without cruelty.

Brontes believes the line only matters if it protects the vulnerable behind it.

## Appearance and Presence

Brontes is broad-shouldered, work-worn, and built like someone who has carried too many people through smoke. Their jacket is reinforced with carbon-black plating and furnace bronze panels. Hazard-gold tape marks old repair seams. A three-headed Cerberus helm patch is stitched over the heart.

They do not posture. They count exits.

## Backstory

Brontes came from the labor warrens under the automated ports, where exosuits replaced unions and workplace injuries were billed as equipment misuse.

After a worker was crushed by a loading rig and charged for the damage, Brontes organized a dockside strike. For twelve hours, three freight arteries froze. For twelve hours, the city remembered workers could still stop the machine.

Then the feeds erased it.

The Surface Order called the strike a false report generated by hostile labor bots. Private security moved in. Brontes did not lead a glorious charge. They led a retreat that saved elders, kids, medics, and the injured.

That became Grinder doctrine.

Hold the line. Carry your people. No one gets left behind because they were not useful enough.

## Escapades

### Dock Nine

Brontes held a port gate with twenty-seven workers, two forklifts, improvised shields, and a prayer to no god in particular. The goal was not victory. The goal was nine more minutes for the evacuation convoy.

They got eleven.

### The Stairwell Mercy

During a housing sweep, Brontes stopped a young Grinder from beating a captured private security officer. The officer had information. More than that, the officer was already beaten.

Brontes said:

> We are not them just because we can be.

The line became mandatory Grinder training.

### The Weight Route

Brontes designed a supply system based on what each volunteer could actually carry, including disabled and elderly operatives. The Surface expected strength to look like force. The Grinders made strength look like everyone arriving with dignity intact.

## Weaknesses and Tensions

- Carries survivor guilt from erased labor actions
- Can mistake rest for abandonment of duty
- Slow to trust abstract strategy
- Protective to the point of stubbornness
- Tension with Veil over injury culture
- Tension with Circuit over being too readable to prediction systems

## Faction Relationships

- **Heathen:** Trust built through evacuation routes and hard truths.
- **Asclepian Veil:** The Veil keeps the Grinders alive. Ione keeps Brontes honest.
- **Oracular Circuit:** Operational respect, tactical unease.
- **Daedalus Foundry:** Relies on their hardware, breaks half of it in field conditions.
- **Styx Rats:** Protective older-sibling energy. The Rats start fires. Brontes gets them home.

## Dossier Quote

> A gate is not holy because it is closed. A gate is holy when it keeps your people alive long enough to see morning.

# 5. Mara Kallix

## Dead Index ID

`DI-0401-MARA-KALLIX`

## Public Handle

**Mara Kallix**

## Known Aliases

- Labyrinth Architect
- Forge-Mother
- Circuit Smith
- Daedalus Prime
- The Woman Who Taught Doors to Lie

## Faction Alignment

**The Daedalus Foundry**

## Rank or Function

Founder and leader of the Daedalus Foundry. Engineer, inventor, hardware hacker, repair strategist, builder of exits.

## Profile Summary

Mara Kallix built pieces of the smart-city cage before she understood the cage had no exit. Now she builds exits, hides doors, prints keys, hardens shelters, repairs dignity, and leaves tyrants trapped inside their own labyrinth.

She believes documentation is a love language and prototypes are confessions.

## Appearance and Presence

Mara dresses like a forge tech who slept under a workbench and woke up with a better idea. Black iron coveralls, brass linework, blueprint-blue projection lenses, molten orange welding scars on her gloves. A mechanical wing folded into a labyrinth is printed across her back, with a tiny key hidden at the center.

She smells faintly of solder, rainwater, and burnt coffee.

## Backstory

Mara designed compliance hardware for smart cities.

Door sensors. Transit gates. Apartment locks. Clinic kiosks. School scanners. Public benefit terminals. She thought better design could make bad systems less cruel. The system disagreed. Every sensor became another brick. Every convenience became a checkpoint. Every maintenance update became a leash.

She tried to sabotage her own designs internally, but the labyrinth adapted faster than conscience.

Heathen found her after she leaked a maintenance map hidden inside a children’s maze puzzle. The first Foundry operation opened service doors in a luxury tower for twenty-three minutes. The Veil extracted seven people. The Rats stole decorative fish from the lobby fountain. Mara laughed once and then cried for what her tools had done before they learned freedom.

## Escapades

### The Door That Should Not Have Opened

Mara built a maintenance ghost that convinced a tower security system it was performing a firmware rollback. For twenty-three minutes, every service door treated the underworld like authorized personnel.

### The Wingless Flight

The Foundry built a rooftop-to-rooftop assistive evacuation rig for wheelchair users trapped above a flooded district. Mara refused to call it wings until the first user did.

### The Tyrant’s Toy Box

Mara released a repair zine that turned discarded corporate home assistants into mesh repeaters, white-noise emitters, and tiny household liars. The Surface issued a recall. No one complied.

## Weaknesses and Tensions

- Guilt over tools she built for the Surface
- Perfectionism disguised as responsibility
- Can disappear into prototypes instead of asking for help
- Underestimates emotional fallout from technical fixes
- Tension with Circuit over deployment risk
- Tension with Rats over breaking tools for spectacle

## Faction Relationships

- **Heathen:** Co-conspirator in impossible infrastructure.
- **Asclepian Veil:** Deep alliance. Builds med kits, assistive tech, privacy devices, mobile clinics.
- **Oracular Circuit:** Secure friction. Cassian audits Mara’s miracles.
- **Myrmidon Grinders:** Field testing by destruction. Brontes breaks what needs strengthening.
- **Styx Rats:** Infuriating, brilliant, constantly stealing prototypes for art crimes.

## Dossier Quote

> If a system has no exit, it is not a system. It is a tomb with a user agreement.

## Rank or Function

Founder and leader of the Styx Rats. Culture jammer, scout, artist, saboteur, morale worker, rumor runner, public humiliation specialist.

## Profile Summary

Rhea Spite understands that fear wants the only microphone. She steals it, paints it pink, makes it play memorial songs, and drops it into the river.

The Surface Order calls her a vandal. The underworld calls her morale infrastructure.

## Appearance and Presence

Rhea looks like a neon warning sign learned to laugh. Black denim, torn mesh, acid-green laces, scratched chrome jewelry, ferryman coin in a tooth setting, rat-skull halo patch, hot-pink paint on her fingers. Her jacket is sticker-bombed with dead gods, strike slogans, QR ghosts, fake ads, and tiny pomegranates with teeth.

She enters rooms like an alarm and somehow makes everyone breathe easier.

## Backstory

Rhea grew up in the ad-choked entertainment districts, where every feeling had a sponsor and every rebellion was sold back as merch before the blood dried.

She learned early that spectacle was not shallow. It was infrastructure. The Surface used spectacle to make power feel inevitable. Rhea used spectacle to make power look stupid.

Her first major hijack replaced a luxury perfume campaign with unpaid medical invoices, missing worker names, and a god-icon vomiting coins. The clip spread through mirrors, fridges, buses, school tablets, and one executive sleep monitor.

When the Thunder Casket broadcast tried to turn annihilation into content, Rhea made the gods into toddlers with lightning forks.

People laughed while crying.

That was when the Rats became necessary.

## Escapades

### The Funeral Carnival

Rhea led the illegal memorial for people erased by the Ledger Purges. Shopping kiosks printed names. Escalators played strike songs. Perfume diffusers released pomegranate and smoke. Security arrived ready for a riot and found people dancing with photographs of the dead.

### The Thunder Lunchbox

During the Thunder Casket Crisis, Rhea and the Rats hijacked war billboards and turned the divine-war relic into a lunchbox with bite marks. The countdown clock became a memorial roll. The slogan read:

**NO GOD GETS TO THREATEN THE SKY AND CALL IT PEACE.**

### The Sponsored Rebellion Trap

Rhea baited a Cloud-Titan lifestyle platform into launching a fake resistance fashion line, then swapped every product page with dossiers of labor abuses, surveillance contracts, and a tutorial for blocking ad trackers.

## Weaknesses and Tensions

- Uses humor to avoid grief
- Can escalate faster than allies can protect
- Hates waiting, especially for security review
- Has trouble respecting quiet forms of resistance
- Tension with Circuit over opsec
- Tension with Veil when spectacle risks patients

## Faction Relationships

- **Heathen:** Calls them graveyard modem. Would burn a city block to save them. Would deny it.
- **Asclepian Veil:** Needs them more than she admits. Ione terrifies her.
- **Oracular Circuit:** Cassian calls her reckless. Rhea calls Cassian “spreadsheet Cassandra.”
- **Myrmidon Grinders:** Favorite protectors. Brontes gets the Rats home when the joke grows teeth.

# Hades Watch Mission Pack II: Underwatch Civic Action Protocols

## Overview

**Mission Pack Theme:** Underwatch Civic Action Protocols  
**Parent Alliance:** The Chthonic Uprising  
**Field Authority:** Underwatch Mission Record  
**Narrative Custodian:** Heathen / The Archivist / Slewfoot  
**Source Status:** Provisional conversion layer from activist checklist research and adjacent civic action guides

This mission pack expands Hades Watch from first-readiness work into safe, legal, nonviolent civic action loops. It converts the outline themes from the provisional activist checklist research into Hades Watch mission pages that can later be seeded as database quests, badge records, profile dossier placeholders, faction reputation rewards, and admin-reviewed proof packets.

The mission families in this pack are built around everyday activism, civic participation, campaign strategy, writing and media literacy, visual activism, data literacy, community service, protest and movement archiving, and nonviolent field preparation.

The research outline could not certify a direct page-for-page extraction from the target checklist site. Treat this pack as **provisional mission design**. When a direct export or browser-capable copy of the target checklist becomes available, exact source checklist names and items can be swapped into the metadata without changing the Hades Watch mission structure.

All missions in this pack are **nonviolent**.

Any approved user from any faction can complete any support mission. Cross-faction work builds faction standing and alliance trust.

Badges display on the operative profile dossier as badge records. Full mission completion requires all required badges listed for that mission. Badge images can begin as square placeholders with clear text labels, faction accent colors, and simple sigil marks until custom art is added.

The Chthonic Uprising does not train operatives to become the violence of the Surface Order. It trains them to keep people alive, connected, informed, prepared, and difficult to erase.

## Hard Nonviolence Rule

All Hades Watch missions must be nonviolent, legal, ethical, and community-centered.

Do not create mission objectives, proof requirements, badges, rewards, or implied paths involving weapons, combat, fighting, physical confrontation, property destruction, harassment, intimidation, stalking, doxxing, threats, real-world sabotage, illegal hacking, law enforcement evasion, unsafe protest tactics, unsafe medical practice, or harm against people, animals, or infrastructure.

In-universe language may sound sharp, mythic, and defiant. Actual mission mechanics must reward only safe civic resilience, care, lawful participation, documentation, education, privacy literacy, creative morale, repair, accessibility, and nonviolent preparation.

## Source Confidence Model

Because this pack is based on a provisional conversion report rather than a direct certified extraction of the target checklist site, each mission includes a `Source Confidence` line.

Recommended values:

- `direct`: use only after exact checklist page text is verified
- `adjacent`: based on accessible adjacent guides with similar task families
- `inferred`: derived from the normalized mission architecture and should be reviewed before public release

For this pack, use `adjacent` or `inferred` until direct source verification is complete.

## Mission 01: Signal Hearth

**Faction Supported:** The Styx Rats  
**Slug:** `signal-hearth`  
**Difficulty:** Initiate

**Source Confidence:** Adjacent  
**Nonviolence Classification:** Nonviolent, community connection and reflection only

### Mission Brief

The Surface Order wants every concern to feel lonely.

The Styx Rats know loneliness is a containment field. Signal Hearth breaks it gently. One cause. One small action. One local connection. One follow-up that does not need applause to matter.

This is not a spectacle mission. It is a hearth mission.

Warm signal. Low flame. Hard to extinguish.

### Real World Purpose

This mission helps operatives convert vague concern into small, repeatable civic habits. It rewards reflection, local connection, and follow-through rather than performative posting.

### In-Universe Context

The Rats are not only billboard ghosts and neon mockery. They are connective tissue. Rumor runners. Morale workers. The ones who know which kitchen needs help, which meeting was missed, which neighbor has been screaming into the void, and which small signal could keep someone from quitting.

### Objective

Choose one cause or community issue, identify three small next actions, connect with one local or mission-aligned group, and log one follow-up.

### Required Badges

| Badge | Slug | Requirement | Proof Type | Required for Completion | Profile Placeholder |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

### Operative Tasks

1. Pick one cause, issue, or community need that matters to you.
2. Write a private reflection on why it matters and what would help.
3. Choose three small next actions that are legal, safe, and realistic.
4. Identify one local organization, mutual aid group, public institution, campaign, or community space connected to the issue.
5. Take one low-risk follow-up action, such as subscribing to updates, attending a public info session, sharing a public resource, volunteering through official channels, or making a respectful inquiry.
6. Submit a redacted proof packet.

### Submission Requirements

The operative submits a short cause summary, three concrete next actions, the type of group or institution contacted, one redacted follow-up note, and confirmation that no private personal data about others is included.

### Proof and Privacy Notes

Journaling can remain private. The site should only collect a short redacted summary. Do not require screenshots of private messages, mailing list emails, group chats, or contact details.

### Safety and Nonviolence Notes

This mission rewards low-risk connection and civic follow-through. It does not involve confrontation, harassment, intimidation, illegal activity, property damage, or public targeting.

### Success Criteria

A reviewer or automated checklist can mark the mission complete when the operative provides a cause summary, three concrete next actions, one connection or resource identified, one follow-up action, and no sensitive information.

The Surface Order trained us to confuse scale with worth. A lantern is not useless because it is not the sun.

## Mission 06: Pulse Survey

**Faction Supported:** The Oracular Circuit  
**Slug:** `pulse-survey`  
**Difficulty:** Specialist  
**Mission Type:** Data Literacy / Consent-Based Survey / Recommendation Writing  
**Estimated Time:** 3 to 5 hours  
**Visibility:** Approved Users

**Faction Reputation Reward:** +35

**Source Confidence:** Adjacent  
**Nonviolence Classification:** Nonviolent, consent-based data literacy only

### Mission Brief

The Surface Order collects everything and understands nothing.

Pulse Survey teaches the opposite: collect little, ask clearly, protect people, summarize humbly, and turn findings into one useful recommendation.

Data can be care when consent is real and the archive knows when to forget.

### Real World Purpose

This mission teaches basic consent-based data literacy. Operatives create a small voluntary survey, collect non-sensitive responses, anonymize findings, and write one recommendation.

### In-Universe Context

The Circuit studies hostile prediction engines, but it also teaches better habits. Not every dataset needs to become a cage. Some can become a mirror, used briefly and handled carefully.

### Objective

Design and complete a small consent-based survey or feedback collection with anonymized findings and one recommendation.

### Required Badges

| Badge | Slug | Requirement | Proof Type | Required for Completion | Profile Placeholder |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

1. Pick a low-risk question related to community needs, accessibility, media habits, resource awareness, or support preferences.
2. Write a short consent statement.
3. Collect only minimal, non-sensitive data.
4. Summarize findings without identifying participants.
5. Write one recommendation based on the findings.
6. Delete or secure raw data according to the consent statement.

### Submission Requirements

The operative submits survey question or feedback prompt, consent statement, data minimization note, anonymized findings, one recommendation, and note on raw data deletion or secure retention.

This mission does not reward surveillance, scraping, coercive research, doxxing, targeting, or exposure. Participation must be voluntary and low-risk.

A reviewer can mark the mission complete when consent language is included, data collection is minimal and low-risk, findings are anonymized, one recommendation is included, and no sensitive raw data is submitted.

**Cassian Nyx, Oracle-Breaker**
